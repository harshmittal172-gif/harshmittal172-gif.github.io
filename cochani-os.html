Case № 01 — Cochani OS
Case Study № 01

Five sites running on paper notebooks.
An operating system, built from the floor.

How a £1.3M retail business with sixty-percent reporting compliance, twelve-percent conversion, and four staff leaving every year became something else entirely — through a system that nobody asked me to build.

Role
Regional Sales & Operations Manager
Duration
2023 — Present, ongoing
Stack
Excel · Power BI · Python · LLM API
Chapter I The Inheritance

I started at Cochani London on seventy pounds a day, as a sales assistant.

The business ran on instinct. Sales were written into paper notebooks at the end of each shift. Stock was ordered on a warehouse manager's memory of what moved last week. Wages were recorded somewhere; sometimes correctly. There was no customer feedback, no KPI tracking, no inventory management. Four staff left every year because there was nothing to grow toward.

Nobody asked me to fix it. I couldn't leave it alone.

i / iv
Chapter II The Diagnosis

Six operational failures, all visible at once.

The reporting compliance sat at 60% — and even that 60% was data nobody trusted. Conversion across five kiosks averaged 12%. Average transaction value was stagnant. Without forecasting, the warehouse over-ordered slow movers and under-ordered fragrances that were quietly selling out by Saturday.

The deeper problem was structural: a fragmented business cannot be coached, only managed by exception. So I designed the fix as a single integrated platform — not a dashboard layered on broken data, but the data layer itself rebuilt underneath.

ii / iv
Chapter III The System

Cochani OS — six modules, one operating layer.

Built in Excel and Power BI for the core data model, extended with Python for automation and an LLM API for narrative reporting. Designed to solve the problems in the order that mattered to the floor.

M—01
Inventory Management
Automated restock triggers per location, controlling shrinkage and ending stockouts on top sellers.
M—02
Demand Forecasting
Trend analysis per location — replacing weekly guesswork with what each kiosk actually moves.
M—03
KPI Tracking
Each staff member measured on the metrics they actually controlled. Visible to them, not just to me.
M—04
Sales Dashboards
Daily, weekly, by location. Live. The business saw itself for the first time.
M—05
Customer Feedback
Lightweight feedback capture for retention — closing the loop with regulars who matter most.
M—06
Staff Portal
Live wages, bonuses, pending payments. Payroll disputes ended the week it shipped.

Chapter IV The Reflection

The business runs on it now.

Cochani OS is still the operating layer of the company. The team has grown from sixteen people to twenty, and turnover has effectively ended — not because of a retention scheme, but because the system makes growth visible and pay transparent, and people stay where they can see themselves winning.

What I learned is more durable than any of the modules: the best operators don't delegate the technical work to someone else. They build the thing themselves, badly at first, well by the end. The credibility you earn doing that is the only currency a team really trusts.
