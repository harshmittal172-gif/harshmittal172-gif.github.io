Case № 03 — The Q3 Alignment
Case Study № 03 · Regal Agencies, Delhi

A campaign succeeded too well. Four teams blamed each other.

How a Q3 marketing promotion that generated extraordinary demand created a fulfilment crisis across forty people in four departments — and how one alignment workshop turned it into a record quarter.

Role
Sales & Marketing Manager
Period
2020 — 2022
Teams
Sales · Marketing · Operations · Accounts
The crisisSuccess, breaking the seams

The marketing campaign worked. Then everything else stopped.

Marketing launched an aggressive Q3 discount and demand surged. Sales, eager to close the bigger deals, started cutting bespoke variations of the promotion. Operations couldn't fulfil non-standard orders fast enough. Accounts drowned in billing discrepancies because the custom discounts weren't syncing between the CRM and the accounting system. Frustrated customers. Four departments pointing at each other.

i / ii
The interventionOne workshop, three commitments

I ran an alignment workshop with the leads from all four teams.

Mapping the friction across functions took half a morning. The compromise that came out of it had three parts: Sales and Marketing agreed to a strict pre-approved menu of three customisable options, killing infinite variations. Operations committed to a guaranteed 48-hour fulfilment SLA for those three options. Accounts built dedicated billing codes so the standardised packages could be automated.

The operational backlog cleared in five days. Billing errors dropped by over ninety percent the following week. The campaign scaled cleanly, and the quarter closed at 120% of revenue target — without burning out the people who had to deliver it.
