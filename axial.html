Case № 04 — Axial 3D
Case Study № 04 · MBA Consulting Project

A pricing engine for medical 3D printing — seventy formulas, two product lines, one scorecard.

Working with Axial 3D, a Belfast-based medical technology firm, I built a B2B pricing decision engine and identified two underserved segments their existing commercial strategy had quietly missed.

The briefStandardise B2B quoting

Two product lines, inconsistent quoting, leaking margin.

Axial 3D's B2B sales team was quoting custom — every deal priced from scratch by the person closest to the customer. That made margin unpredictable and made it impossible to compare deals across the pipeline. The brief was: standardise the quoting process without slowing it down.

i / ii
The buildEngine plus scorecard

Seventy live formulas. A seven-factor deal scorecard. Two segments found.

The pricing engine, built in Excel, modelled cost build-up across both product lines with seventy live formulas — the salesperson now enters configuration, and the engine returns a defensible price and margin in seconds. The deal scorecard weights seven commercial and strategic factors to give each pipeline opportunity a comparable score.

The competitive gap analysis surfaced two underserved B2B segments — NHS surgical units and medical-device R&D teams — that became the basis of a segment-specific go-to-market strategy.
