A short biographical note

Commercial operator. Builder.
MBA candidate at Brunel.

Portrait
portrait.jpg
4 : 5 ratio

I started at Cochani London on seventy pounds a day, as a sales assistant — first in my family to move abroad, no network to lean on. Eighteen months later I was running five sites and a team of twenty, having engineered the operational system the business runs on today.

I'm not satisfied when a team is merely compliant; I'm satisfied when it's winning. The same instinct that won't leave a broken process alone is the one that shapes how I lead. I don't force adoption from the top — I sit with people one at a time and work the resistance until it sticks.

I'm competitive, but not with the people around me. I'm competitive with the gap between where something is and where I know it could be. That gap is the thing I can't walk away from. It's why I build systems instead of just hitting targets, and why I try to make things that run without me rather than because of me.

I'm finishing my MBA at Brunel University London on an academic scholarship — every exam result so far at distinction. Recent consulting projects with Axial 3D (medical 3D printing) and Transportify (B2B logistics) sit in the Index.

I've never been chasing a salary; I'm chasing work I'd be glad to put my name on.

Hand me a problem that matters.
I'll give it everything I have until it works.
